Barcelona, Sorsogon

A coastal municipality in the province of Sorsogon, Philippines, known for its rich history, stunning coastal views, and a charming town center, particularly recognized for the historic Saint Joseph Parish Church.

History of Barcelona, Sorsogon

The site of Barcelona was originally part of Gubat and Bulusan. The town's name was known as Danlog, taken from the name of a local river. The change of name to Barcelona was recommended by a Spanish official who saw some similarities with Barcelona, Catalonia in Spain. On April 16, 1886, it became a pueblo civil. Until 1868, it was part of the parish of St. James the Great of Bulusan. After 1868, it took St. Joseph as its patron saint and celebrates their town fiesta on May 19.

Events

Events in Barcelona

Hinguha Festival

The Hinguha Festival is a celebration of the cultural heritage and artistic talents of Barcelona, Sorsogon. It takes place during the Provincial Historico-Cultural Parade and Float Competition, which is part of the Kasanggayahan Festival.

Fiesta

The town fiesta is celebrated on May 19 in honor of the town's patron saint, St. Joseph. The fiesta is a week-long celebration that includes various cultural and religious activities, such as processions, masses, and traditional dances. It is a time for the community to come together and celebrate their heritage and faith.
