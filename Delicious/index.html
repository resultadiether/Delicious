Barcelona, Sorsogon

A coastal municipality in the province of Sorsogon, Philippines, known for its rich history, stunning coastal views, and a charming town center, particularly recognized for the historic Saint Joseph Parish Church.

History of Barcelona, Sorsogon

The site of Barcelona was originally part of Gubat and Bulusan. The town's name was known as Danlog, taken from the name of a local river. The Danlog tributary and coastal waters were used for trade with neighboring settlements. The settlement was a tribal community of hunters and fishermen. In 1800s, the Spaniards came and laid claim on Danlog, the settlement was eventually change its name to Barcelona that was recommended by a Spanish official who saw some similarities with Barcelona, Catalonia, Spain. On April 16, 1866, Barcelona was declared as a "pueblo civil" by virtue of a Spanish Royal Decree. Although already declared officially as an independent "pueblo civil", Barcelona was still subjugated as part of the Parish of Bulusan. Saint Joseph the Worker, as the Patron Saint. In 1868, or two years after the issuance of the Royal Decree, Barcelona became an independent town from Bulusan. The people celebrates their town fiesta in honor of Saint Joseph the Worker every May 19, which remains to be so until now.

Events in Barcelona

Hinguha Festival

The Hinguha Festival is a celebration of the cultural heritage and artistic talents of Barcelona, Sorsogon. It takes place during the Provincial Historico-Cultural Parade and Float Competition, which is part of the Kasanggayahan Festival. This festival celebrates the rich cultural heritage and artistic talents of the local community, showcasing traditional music, dance, and other art forms. The term "hinguha" is derived from the Bicolano word meaning "effort" or "strive," reflecting the community's dedication to preserving and promoting their cultural identity. The festival serves as a platform for the people of Barcelona to express their creativity and unity, fostering a sense of pride and continuity in their traditions. In October 2024, the Hinguha Festival was featured as part of the 50th Kasanggayahan Festival's Provincial Historico-Cultural Street Dancing Competition in Sorsogon City. Performers from Barcelona participated in this event, highlighting their unique cultural expressions through vibrant street dances and musical performances. The festival aims to promote cultural awareness and appreciation among the local community and visitors, fostering a sense of pride and unity in the shared heritage of the region.

Fiesta

The town fiesta is celebrated on May 19 in honor of the town's patron saint, St. Joseph. The fiesta is a week-long celebration that includes various cultural and religious activities, such as processions, masses, and traditional dances. It is a time for the community to come together and celebrate their heritage and faith. These observances are held at the historic St. Joseph Church, a well-preserved Spanish-era structure that stands as a symbol of Barcelona’s rich heritage. One of the highlights is the "Pista sa Nayon," where families prepare traditional Bicolano delicacies like laing, pinangat, and Bicol Express, welcoming guests into their homes to share in the feast. The celebration not only strengthens the faith of the townspeople but also serves as a homecoming event for many, fostering deep connections among families and friends. With its mix of religious devotion, cultural pride, and warm hospitality, the Barcelona Town Fiesta remains one of the most anticipated events in the municipality, drawing visitors who wish to experience the town’s historical charm and festive spirit.
